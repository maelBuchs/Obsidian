##### Definition of a [[Map]]

If also $\forall x \in A: \exists y \in B : (x,y)\in G_{f}$ is true, we write:
$$f:A\rightarrow B \text{ and } f(x)=y \text{ for } (x,y)\in G_{f}$$
$f$ is a [[Map]] (and a [[function]] in certain contexts) from $A$ to $B$.(map : `f[x] = y `, function: `f(x) = y`)
$G$ is the [[graph]] of $f$.
$A$ is the [[domain]] of $f$ and $B$ is the [[codomain]] of $f$.

*For example*
$$\begin{gathered}

f: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \\
x \mapsto x^2
\end{gathered}$$
